Lifestyle

The growth of cities were caused by the rapid increase in population. While cities expanded, the living conditions decreased. Cities had unpaved streets that were filled with garbage. Many diseases such as cholera spread as families shared one room. Working conditions were just as bad as they had very long shifts, an unsanitary environment and was quit unsafe. Women and children were sent as coal miners, as a result they took in coal dust introduced coal lung also known as black lung disease. During this time period, the Middle Class had thrived as they were most nessecary fields. These jobs included factory mangers, government employees, doctors, and lawyers. The Working Class were slowly being taken over by machines, as a result the Luddites realiated by destroyed many factories.
The health of the working class families in England were harmed by these textile factories. Conveyed through the interview with John Birley during 1849, who had undergone this difficult working conditions, stated that he was beaten senseless, worked long hard shifts, and were served low quality food.

Developments



Britain

Britain was the first country to industrialize as it had many advantages over other countries in Europe. There were an abundance of natural resources and had a stable government that supported this change. The government encouraged internal trade and helped with the building of roads, canals, and harbors. It also provided stability among consumers and sellers. This allowed Britain to have a monopoly on inventions as they forced colonies to buy goods that were produced by their factories.
